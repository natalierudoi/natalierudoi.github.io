Teaching Philosophy

I believe that every student of mine is unique and capable of rising to a high level of performance with self-motivation, discipline, and hard work.  I have had many years of experience teaching students of every age and level. From beginning to advanced players, I find creative ways to help each student reach his or her goals. Whether they have goals of becoming a professional musician or simply want to learn a new skill or hobby, I put the same amount of focus and attention into each individual.

My method varies determined on the age and level of each student. For beginning students, I combine Suzuki method training with my own traditional method. For the more advanced student, my method is based on the teaching principles of Ivan Galamian. My goal as a teacher is to establish a firm technical foundation that allows for freedom of artistic expression for each individual student. I believe that music is a language and an effective way of communicating the thoughts of one’s heart and mind to the listener. Each student has their own voice, and it is my goal to help them express that voice with confidence and joy. As the great cellist Pablo Casals once said, “Music is the divine way to tell beautiful, poetic things to the heart.” This is the center of my philosophy that I will instill in each of my students. Teaching has been and always will be a privilege for me as I help my students learn and grow in this beautiful form of self-expression and share with them my passion for music.

Natalie Rudoi currently teaches violin at Cairn University, the Csehy Summer School of Music, and also maintains a private studio in New Jersey. Her students have been members of the Philadelphia Youth Orchestra, Philadelphia Sinfonia, and have also been first prize winners in local competitions.
